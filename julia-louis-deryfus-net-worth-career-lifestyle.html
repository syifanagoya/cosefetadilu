Julia Louis-Dreyfus: Career

Julia Louis-Dreyfus has had a pretty successful career. Initially, she dropped out before graduation to pursue a career in acting.

As part of her training, she appeared in Chicago’s improv group, The Second City.

At the young age of 21, she landed a part on Saturday Night Live. She was the youngest female cast in SNL’s history and stayed there for roughly 3 years.

Towards the end of her SNL Career, she met Larry David, the guy who co-created Seinfield.

After leaving the show, she got the chance to appear in high-profile movies including, Hannah and Her Sisters by Woody Allen.

Her fame skyrocketed when she joined the cast of Seinfeld in the early 90s.

Originally, her character wasn’t supposed to be on the show, but through Elaine, she eventually won the hearts of millions.

She was nominated and eventually won several prestigious awards for her outstanding performance, including the Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress.

After Seinfield, for a while, she found very little success. Most of the shows never made it past pilots or were really short-lived.

She again found success in 2005, when she was cast in the CBS sitcom “The New Adventures of Old Christine.” 

Again, for her great performance, she earned numerous nominations and won an Emmy too.

Her next big hit came in 2012 when she joined the cast of Veep. The show was appreciated by a lot and was one of the most critically acclaimed shows in HBO.

Julia was so good that she won 6 consecutive Emmy awards for portraying the vice president.

Lately, she has appeared in 2 marvel projects, i.e., Black Widow and The Falcon and The Winter Soldier.

It is highly likely that she will continue her prospects in the TV industry in the future as well.
